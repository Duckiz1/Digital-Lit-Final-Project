Section 1

What is Online Privacy?

Online privacy is your right to control personal information while using the internet—what is collected, how it is used, and who can access it. Data protection enforces these rights through tools like encryption.

Key Idea

Privacy vs. Protection

Privacy defines who should access your data; protection uses safeguards to prevent unauthorized access.

Section 2

Why Does It Matter?

Without proper privacy measures, your data can be exploited for identity theft, scams, or misinformation. Protecting data keeps you in control of your choices and security.

Real-world Impact

  • Identity theft and financial loss
  • Manipulation through targeted misinformation
  • Constant tracking that shapes decisions without consent

Section 3

Common Threats and Risks

Watch for hackers, phishing, and companies over-collecting or sharing data without consent.

Threat

Phishing & Scams

Suspicious links and fake sites that steal credentials or banking info.

How to spot: Look for urgent language, misspelled URLs, and requests for personal info.

Threat

Device Breaches

Malware, weak passwords, or unsecured Wi‑Fi that let attackers in.

Prevention: Use strong passwords, keep software updated, and avoid public Wi-Fi for sensitive tasks.

Threat

Excessive Tracking

Apps and big tech collecting more data than necessary, sometimes sharing with third parties.

Take action: Review app permissions, use privacy-focused browsers, and opt out of data sharing.
